Why is E-Waste recycling Important?

this is how important e-waste recycling is.

Data security involves safeguarding digital information from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. It includes practices such as encryption, access controls, authentication, and regular monitoring to ensure data confidentiality, integrity, and availability. Compliance with regulations, employee training, and security policies are essential components. Effective data security is vital for protecting sensitive information, mitigating data breaches, and maintaining trust with customers and partners.

Public awareness in e-waste recycling is crucial for educating individuals and businesses about the environmental and health impacts of improper disposal. Awareness campaigns, workshops, and educational programs inform people about the importance of recycling electronics, reducing electronic waste, and the availability of collection centers. Encouraging responsible e-waste disposal practices empowers the public to make informed choices, contributing to a sustainable and eco-conscious society..

Resource recovery in e-waste recycling involves reclaiming valuable materials from discarded electronic devices. Through various processes like dismantling, shredding, and smelting, materials such as gold, silver, copper, and rare earth metals are extracted for reuse. This not only conserves precious natural resources but also reduces the environmental impact of mining and reduces the energy consumption associated with raw material extraction, promoting a more sustainable and eco-friendly approach to electronics manufacturing. .

Global initiatives in e-waste management involve international organizations and agreements like the Basel Convention. These initiatives aim to address the cross-border movement of hazardous e-waste, promote responsible recycling practices, and harmonize regulations among countries. They facilitate cooperation in managing the growing e-waste problem, protect the environment, and reduce health risks associated with electronic waste.

The timeline

number of e-waste generated and recycled worldwide by year

  • 2019

    A Global Crisis in the Making!

    still increasing

    In 2019, approximately 53.6 million metric tons (Mt) of e-waste (excluding PV panels) was generated, or 7.3 kg per capita. It is estimated that the amount of e-waste generated will exceed 74Mt in 2030. Thus, the global quantity of e-waste is increasing at an alarming rate of almost 2 Mt per year. In 2019, the large majority of e-waste generated (82.6%) was most likely not formally collected and not managed in an environmental sound manner.

  • 2021

    YAyy! 99876 105 Kg waste recycled

    still increasing

    It is estimated that 57.4 Mt (Million Metric Tonnes) of e-waste was generated globally in 2021. Less than 1/5 is recycled globally, although this varies by region. Only 17.4% of total global e-waste is known to have been collected and properly recycled. This figure has fallen in the last 5 years as a percentage of total waste generated. .

  • 2023

    E-Waste Recycle Global market rate went pew!pew!

    and of course still increasing

    The global E-scrap recycling market grew from $7.77 billion in 2022 to $8.71 billion in 2023, exhibiting a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 12.1%. Despite the disruptions caused by the Russia-Ukraine war, which impacted global economic recovery from the COVID-19 pandemic, the e-scrap recycling market is projected to reach $13.05 billion in 2027 at a CAGR of 10.6%.
